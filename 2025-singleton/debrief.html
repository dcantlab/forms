Influence of Salient Distractor on Focused and Distributed Attention — Background and Purpose of the Study

Background. People use attention to process visual information efficiently within the limitations of the visual system. Attention can be focused on certain parts (focused attention), but it can also be distributed over wide spatial range (distributed attention). People process information efficiently by varying the spatial distribution of attention according to the information to be processed. However, sometimes they unintentionally direct attention to items that are not related to the task (Theeuwes, 1994). In visual scenes, there are various distractors that interfere with the processing. To date, there have been no studies on how these distractors affect the spatial distribution of attention.

Purpose. The present study intend to investigate how distractor affects the spatial distribution of attention.

References.

  • Theeuwes, J. (1994). Stimulus-driven capture and attentional set: Selective search for color and visual abrupt onsets. Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Perception and Performance, 20(4), 799–806.
